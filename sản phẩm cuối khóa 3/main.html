Study content

In the introductory phase, students begin their studies in small teams with a specific project. Various courses provide the basic foundations of the subject, and cross-disciplinary approaches are used to establish practical examples. Excursions to regional companies provide insight into IT careers. After further introductory courses, students gain insight into their future careers during the practical semester. Alternatively, they can gain international experience during a semester abroad in the fourth semester. Starting in the fifth semester, elective modules delve deeper into various key topics in computer science. The project and the bachelor's thesis conclude the program.

Focus on IT systems integration: These days, software systems aren't developed in a vacuum, but are typically integrated into an existing system landscape. For this reason, we offer a focus on IT systems integration at Bremerhaven University of Applied Sciences. Project orientation: Project-oriented learning from the first to the last semester creates a pleasant study atmosphere and solid career preparation. Extracurricular learning: In extracurricular working groups, such as the infrastructure group, interested students and teachers work together to further develop IT infrastructure and research the latest open source technologies.

By completing an internship semester early on, students gain insight into everyday working life and can thus base their further studies on a more solid understanding of their future career path. At their internship company, students build contacts and develop specific career prospects. Alternatively, they can complete an internship or study abroad program, which contributes to the personal development of students through the acquisition of intercultural skills and international practical or study experience. Study abroad programs are supported through partnerships between Bremerhaven University of Applied Sciences and partner universities abroad, as well as through scholarship opportunities.

Admission and application

The prerequisite for admission to the program is a general higher education entrance qualification, a university of applied sciences entrance qualification, or an equivalent recognized educational qualification. In addition, since spring 2024, a three-year degree program leads to a subject-specific higher education entrance qualification. For questions regarding admission, and in particular regarding the possibilities of studying without a higher education entrance qualification, please contact the Admissions Office and the Student Advisory Service. For international students, a C1 level of German is required.

The Computer Science (INF) program accepts students annually for the winter semester. The application deadline is August 15th. Applications are submitted online via our ecampus application portal , where all required documents must be uploaded. Bremerhaven University of Applied Sciences is a member university of UNI-ASSIST (International Student Application Service Center). Prospective students who obtained their university entrance qualification outside of the European Union (EU) should therefore submit their applications directly to UNI-ASSIST . To ensure sufficient processing time, applications for the winter semester should be submitted to UNI-ASSIST by June 30th. After successful review, they will be forwarded to Bremerhaven University of Applied Sciences. Applicants will be charged a fee for UNI-ASSIST's processing of applications. Further information on the application process can be found on the website of the Enrollment and Examination Office.

Before you begin your studies, take advantage of the opportunity to participate in our university-internal preparatory courses and the programs for prospective students.
